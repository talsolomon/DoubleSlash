## Headline Answer

**Yes — but it's split into two distinct shapes, and neither is "Fiverr for AI builders" cleanly.**

1. **Talent marketplaces** (the literal Fiverr pattern) — sell *hours of a human* who builds AI for you.
2. **Agent / asset marketplaces** (the app-store pattern) — sell *pre-built agents/skills/templates* you install or invoke.

The two camps barely overlap. The "buy a vetted human who builds you a custom agent fitted to your context" niche is partly served, but discovery, vetting, and outcome-pricing are weak across the board.

## Landscape Map

### A. Talent marketplaces — hire humans to build AI

| Platform | Shape | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Fiverr** | Open marketplace + Fiverr Pro | Dedicated **AI Agent Development** category; gigs $120–$140 typical, specialists charge much more. Covers GPT-4 chat/voice agents, n8n + VAPI + Relevance AI builds, REST/webhook workflows. Has a separate Fiverr Pro AI Experts vertical. |
| **Upwork / Freelancer** | Open marketplace | Wide range of AI-services gigs; less curated than Fiverr Pro. |
| **Toptal** | Vetted (~3%) | Dedicated *AI Engineers* and *Prompt Engineers* sections; ~48-hour match, 98% trial-to-hire. |
| **Arc.dev / Uplers / Softaims / Second Talent / KORE1** | Vetted networks | All advertise 24–72h matching for AI/ML/GenAI specialists. |
| **A.Team** *(adjacent)* | Team-as-a-service | Assembles small AI builder squads, not single freelancers. |

**What's sold:** developer hours, fixed-price gigs, retainers.
**Buyer profile:** PMs, founders, ops leads, small agencies.
**Pricing logic:** time + complexity. No outcome guarantees.

### B. Agent / asset marketplaces — buy or invoke pre-built agents

The "eight that matter" cluster identified by Digital Applied (April 2026):

| Marketplace | Audience | Pricing model |
|---|---|---|
| **GPT Store** (OpenAI) | Consumer + prosumer | Engagement-based revenue share; ~$0.03/conversation. 3M+ GPTs listed. |

| **Vercel Agent Gallery / Marketplace** | Vercel devs | Native integrations — Agents (work) + Services (infra). |
| **Cloudflare AI Marketplace** | Cloudflare devs | Bills on inference. |

**Plus adjacents:**
- **AI Agent Store** (aiagentstore.ai) — directory of agents *and* AI agencies.
- **Picsart Agent Marketplace** (launched March 2026) — creators "hire" AI assistants for resize/remix/Shopify product editing.
- **Kore.ai Marketplace** — enterprise agent catalog.
- **Lindy / Relevance AI / CrewAI / Sider / Stack AI** — agent builders that ship template galleries (not pure marketplaces, but template-distribution channels).

### C. The gap between A and B

Almost nobody sells **"a vetted builder who fits a pre-made agent to your context."** The closest:
- **AI Agent Store**'s agency-list mode (directory only, not transactional).
- **Fiverr Pro**'s AI Experts (closer, but generic gig flow, no agent-asset reuse).

## Buyer/Seller Dynamics

**What's actually selling on talent marketplaces (per Fiverr/Upwork 2026 trend reports):**
- AI voice-calling agents (n8n + VAPI + Relevance AI stack)
- CRM/lead enrichment pipelines
- Custom GPT builds for SMB workflows
- Webhook/API plumbing between LLM + business systems

**What's selling on asset marketplaces:**
- GPT Store: utilities, niche assistants — but creator earnings are weak ($100–$500/mo soft ceiling for most; need 33k+ quality convos for $1k/mo).
- Replit/Vercel/Cloudflare: developer-facing primitives (research agents, deploy-helpers, infra glue).
- Picsart: vertical creator agents (image edit, social resize).

**The smart-money pivot, called out across multiple 2026 sources:** creators are abandoning store-revenue-share and moving to **B2B consulting engagements at $5k–$20k+** — i.e., back to talent-marketplace economics, but selling outcomes not hours.

## Gaps & Positioning Angles (relevant to Duble//Slash)

1. **Discovery is broken on both sides.** Multiple sources flag distribution as the real bottleneck — "shipping the agent is solved; getting it surfaced isn't." A platform that surfaces agents *contextually* (right agent for the buyer's context) rather than by category browse beats current marketplaces.

2. **No "context-fit" layer.** Every agent in GPT Store / Vercel / Replit is sold as a generic artifact. Buyers then spend their own time wiring it to their data, brand, conventions. This is the exact slot Context Cloud occupies.

3. **Monetization is bifurcated and unhappy.**
   - Asset stores pay creators near-nothing.
   - Talent marketplaces commoditize hours.
   - Neither rewards "I built an agent that fits your context and produced outcome X." A marketplace where the unit is **outcome-tied agent + context bundle** is unclaimed.

4. **Designers/PMs are unserved as buyers.** The talent marketplaces lean technical (engineers selling to engineers). The asset marketplaces lean either consumer (GPT Store) or developer (Vercel/Replit/Cloudflare). A buyer who is a designer or PM needing a *production-ready, context-aware* agent has nowhere good to land. This maps directly to your Duble//Slash audience.

5. **Builder-side: identity & portfolio portability.** A Fiverr AI seller's reputation lives only on Fiverr. A GPT Store creator's reputation lives only there. There's no portable "AI builder" identity layer — and no marketplace that lets a builder show *the agents they shipped + the contexts they fit them to* as a unified portfolio.

---

## Implications for Duble//Slash

- **Don't pitch Duble//Slash as "Fiverr for AI."** The literal Fiverr-for-AI slot is occupied by Fiverr itself plus a crowd of vetted networks. You'd be entering a commoditized hours-selling market.
- **The defensible adjacent slot:** the **context layer + agent fabric** that *makes* hired or bought agents actually deliver. That's not a marketplace — it's the substrate marketplaces are missing.
- **If a marketplace ever fits the roadmap**, the right framing is *"a place where designers/PMs commission context-fitted agents,"* with Context Cloud as the moat (the agent doesn't just ship — it ships pre-wired to your context).
- **Pricing precedent worth stealing:** outcome-tied / B2B-engagement pricing ($5k–$20k bundles) over hourly or per-conversation. The market is already pivoting there.
