# Articles

## Purpose

Articles establish methodology credibility. They lower studio pilot friction by making the Fish Model feel real before anyone has seen the product. They are not product announcements.

---

## Topic clusters

| Cluster | Angle | Monetization tie |
|---|---|---|
| **AI + design workflow** | How AI changes (and doesn't change) the design process | Top of funnel for Pro tier; studio credibility |
| **Context collapse** | Why designers lose context between tools, sessions, collaborators — and what that costs | Core problem the product solves; awareness |
| **Fish Model** | Methodology writing — frame, case study, practitioner reflections | Methodology credibility; pilot friction |
| **Running a design studio on AI** | AI-native ops for small studios — practical, not hype | Direct studio cohort pipeline |

---

## Outlets (ranked by fit)

| Outlet | Fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Medium / personal | High | Low friction, indexable, cross-postable |
| UX Collective (Medium pub) | High | Curated; submit after piece is polished |
| Smashing Magazine | Medium | Long lead time; high authority |
| Designer News | Medium | Link-share format; article must stand alone |
| LinkedIn (Tal's profile) | High | Studio buyers are there; post condensed version |

---

## Cadence

2-week rhythm: one article draft per fortnight, one published per month. Quality over frequency.

---

## Agent loop

CMO agent (`AD` skill) drafts outline → Tal reviews → CMO drafts full piece → Tal edits + publishes. Articles require Tal's voice; agent handles structure and first draft only.
