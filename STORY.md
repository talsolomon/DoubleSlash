## OPENING — Mirror their words back

YC described the opportunity perfectly: a connective layer that makes a company legible to AI by default. We're building it. But we started somewhere they haven't yet — with the individual worker.

---

## THE PROBLEM — Precise and earned

A designer at a studio opens her day across five tools: Figma, Jira, Claude, Slack, Notion. She knows what she's trying to do. The AI tools don't. Every session starts from zero. She spends twenty minutes reconstructing context before she can ask the right question. Then she switches to Perplexity, and does it again.

This isn't a productivity problem. It's a continuity problem. And it lives at the level of the individual worker — before it ever reaches the company.

The integration nightmare YC described — Slack, Linear, GitHub, Notion, custom glue code — exists because nobody solved the human layer first. You can't build a company-level intelligence loop if every person inside that company is an open loop.

---

## THE INSIGHT — The one they haven't named

The bottleneck isn't AI capability. Every tool is powerful. The bottleneck is **context continuity** — between tools, between people, between the moment of intent and the moment of execution.

Non-technical users — designers, PMs, creative leads — are the majority of knowledge workers. They understand what needs to be done. They cannot operate AI tools at the level required to make that happen. The gap between their intent and AI's output is context. Nobody is closing that gap at the human level.

---

## THE SOLUTION — No fluff

Duble//Slash is a personal agent that lives on your machine.

You press `//` anywhere — inside Claude, inside Figma, inside Perplexity — and it knows what project you're focused on, what files are relevant, what phase you're in, what was decided last. It injects the right context into the right tool without you rebuilding it from scratch.

A designer types `//` in Figma: *"Create concept designs for the home page."* The agent knows the Figma file, the frame, the brief, the last design review. It doesn't ask. It acts.

A PM types `//` in Claude: *"Initiate competitive research based on our last discussion."* The agent attaches the relevant MD files, the project brief, the open tickets. The prompt is already right.

This is not a wrapper. It is a **workflow layer** that lives where work actually happens.

---

## THE MULTIPLIER — Where it becomes a company

When a team adopts this, something shifts. Context doesn't break between people.

A designer needs a technical review before proceeding. She assigns it to the developer. He opens his agent, pulls the project context, and gives her a grounded technical view — without a three-message Slack thread to reconstruct what they're talking about.

Every brief written through `//`, every review captured, every meeting summarized — the company's artifacts become legible to AI naturally. Not because someone built an integration. Because the workers captured it, one `//` at a time.

This is the closed loop YC described — built from the bottom up.

---

## WHO WE START WITH

Design studios delivering work for clients. R&D teams trying to harness AI without hiring AI engineers. PMs who need to move fast across tools they don't fully control. The non-technical majority — the people who get the least out of AI today and have the most to gain.

---

## WHERE THIS GOES

When agents are present in every worker's workflow, they become present in the team. In the standup. In the sprint review. In the Zoom call — not as a recorder, but as a participant with context. A team where every person's agent knows the project is a team that moves differently. That is the company that becomes queryable by default.

---

## THE ASK

We are at the stage of building our first design studio cohort. We are not asking you to bet on a vision. We are asking you to look at the gap between where AI tools are and where the average knowledge worker actually is — and tell us you don't see it.

We see it. We're closing it.
